# Review Instructions
## SEC Filing Q&A Data Collection

**Date:** April 2026

---

## Hello!

Thanks for your interset in our SEC review task! Some background:

We're building a dataset of Q&A pairs from SEC financial filings (10-K and 10-Q reports) that will be used to fine-tune a large language model on financial reasoning. As a reviewer, you're the quality gate — your job is to catch errors before they make it into the training data. A wrong answer in the dataset doesn't just get delivered to a customer; it teaches the model incorrect financial reasoning. Hold submissions to an expert standard: the kind of answer a senior analyst would stand behind.

Here's what this involves:

1. **What you're reviewing** — Q&A pairs from a completed annotation task. Each one is labeled Easy, Medium, or Hard.
2. **What you're checking** — whether the answer is factually correct, the line of reasoning is sound, and whether the Supporting Facts give enough evidence to verify the answer. Details in [Review Checklist](#review-checklist).
3. **What you earn** — $20/hr base, plus bonuses for Medium and Hard reviews. See [Pay](#pay).

## Getting Started

**Step 1 — Open a task set CSV** from the [shared Google Drive folder](https://drive.google.com/drive/folders/1Z7sXh9cIrBew4_BdCmfhGMAL_Fi2LDF9?usp=drive_link). Pick any set where the `question` and `answer` columns are filled in but the `Rating (for reviewer)` column is empty — that means annotation is complete and the review is unclaimed.

**Step 2 — Read through each row** before assigning any ratings. Get a feel for the overall quality and consistency of the submission before diving into individual corrections.

**Step 3 — For each row**, run through the [Review Checklist](#review-checklist) below. Use the `source_url` link in the row to open the referenced filing wherever you need to verify a claim directly.

**Step 4 — Fill in your rating** in the `Rating (for reviewer)` column. For any rating of 1, also fill in `Explanation (for reviewer)`. See [Rating 1: Making the Correction](#rating-1-making-the-correction) for exactly what to do.

**Step 5 — Log your time** in the `time_spent` column for each row (e.g., "18 min"). We use this for payment — please be accurate!

## Rating Scale

Assign one rating per row:

| Rating | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| 2 — Accept | Accurate, self-contained, correct difficulty level, no personal identifiable information (PII) |
| 1 — Revise | Minor fixable issue; you implement the correction in bold directly in the CSV |
| 0 — Reject | Factually wrong, not self-contained, or missing source |

---

## Review Checklist

Run through this for every Q&A:

**Self-containedness** — does the Supporting Facts section have what it needs?
- *Easy:* page citation present
- *Medium:* all calculation inputs listed with page numbers
- *Hard:* each company-level claim has a cited fact or quoted language with filing and page; if a disclosure is absent, the annotator documented where they looked

**Accuracy** — is what's there correct?
- *Easy:* open the cited page and confirm the fact is stated as claimed
- *Medium:* run the calculation yourself using the Supporting Facts values; flag a mismatch or a misidentified metric (e.g., gross profit used instead of operating income)
- *Hard:*  Check that the synthesis is logically supported by the cited evidence; flag if a conclusion overstates the evidence, a causal claim isn't justified, or an absent disclosure isn't documented

**Difficulty level** — does the Q&A match its Easy / Medium / Hard label?

**PII** — flag and anonymize any personal names, contact info, or identifying details.

## Rating 1: Making the Correction

If you assign a 1, do two things:

1. **Implement the correction** directly in the CSV row. Use **bold text** for every change so the project lead and annotator can see exactly what you altered.
2. **Write your rationale** in the `Explanation (for reviewer)` column — explain exactly what was wrong or missing and why you made the change. Be specific.
