# Review Instructions
## SEC Filing Q&A Data Collection

**Date:** April 2026

---

## Hello!

Thanks for signing up to review — we have a task all set up for you! :)

We're building a dataset of Q&A pairs from SEC financial filings (10-K and 10-Q reports) that will be used to fine-tune a large language model (LLM) on financial reasoning. As a reviewer, you're the quality gate — your job is to catch errors before they make it into the training data. A wrong answer in the dataset doesn't just get delivered to a customer; it teaches the model incorrect financial reasoning. Keep in mind that the Q&As are written for a finance-literate reader, not necessarily a domain expert — evaluate them with that audience in mind.

Here's what this involves:

1. **What you're reviewing** — Q&A pairs from a completed annotation task. Each one is labeled Easy, Medium, or Hard.
2. **What you're checking** — whether the answer is factually correct and whether the Supporting Facts give enough evidence to verify it. Details in [Review Checklist](#review-checklist).
3. **What you earn** — $20/hr base, plus bonuses for Medium and Hard reviews. See [Pay](#pay).

You'll receive the completed CSV for your claimed task set. The `source_url` column in each row links directly to the SEC filing — use these to verify claims where needed.

## Rating Scale

Assign one rating per row:

| Rating | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| 2 — Accept | Accurate, self-contained, correct difficulty level, no personal identifiable information (PII) |
| 1 — Revise | Minor fixable issue; you implement the correction in bold directly in the CSV |
| 0 — Reject | Factually wrong, not self-contained, or missing source |

---

## Review Checklist

Run through this for every Q&A:

**Self-containedness** — does the Supporting Facts section have what it needs?
- *Easy:* page citation present
- *Medium:* all calculation inputs listed with page numbers
- *Hard:* each company-level claim has a cited fact or quoted language with filing and page; if a disclosure is absent, the annotator documented where they looked

**Accuracy** — is what's there correct?
- *Easy:* open the cited page and confirm the fact is stated as claimed
- *Medium:* run the calculation yourself using the Supporting Facts values; flag a mismatch or a misidentified metric (e.g., gross profit used instead of operating income)
- *Hard:*  Check that the synthesis is logically supported by the cited evidence; flag if a conclusion overstates the evidence, a causal claim isn't justified, or an absent disclosure isn't documented

**Difficulty level** — does the Q&A match its Easy / Medium / Hard label?

**PII** — flag and anonymize any personal names, contact info, or identifying details.

## Rating 1: Making the Correction

If you assign a 1, do two things:

1. **Implement the correction** directly in the CSV row. Use **bold text** for every change so the project lead and annotator can see exactly what you altered.
2. **Write your rationale** in the `Explanation (for reviewer)` column — explain exactly what was wrong or missing and why you made the change. Be specific.
