## Getting Started

Your task set is a pre-filled CSV file. Each row is one Q&A slot. If the `question` and `answer` fields are empty, that's a Q&A you need to write.

**Step 1 — Enter your worker ID** in the `annotator_id` column at the top of the CSV.

**Step 2 — Download your filings.** For each of the 3 assigned companies:
1. Click the link in the `sec_url` column — it opens a Google search filtered to SEC.gov for that company and filing type.
2. Find the correct filing on SEC EDGAR (match the filing type and date in your CSV).
3. On the filing page, click **Menu → Open as HTML**. Copy the URL — this is your `sec_page_url`.
4. Press **Ctrl+P** (or **Cmd+P** on Mac), set destination to "Save as PDF", and save using the filename already listed in your CSV (e.g., `Apple_10K_2025.pdf`).

Repeat for all 3 companies. You should have 3 PDFs before writing anything.

**Step 3 — Start your timer.** Keep a running total of your hours as you work — you'll submit this alongside your CSV.

**Step 4 — Write your Q&As.** See [Question Types](#question-types) below for what each category requires.

**Step 5 — Return your completed CSV and all PDFs** (including any extra filings you pulled for Hard questions).

## Question Types

There are three difficulty levels. Your task set CSV has rows pre-labeled Easy, Medium, or Hard — write the Q&A that fits that row's label.

### Easy

A single clearly-stated fact, directly readable from the filing. No calculation or inference needed. Cite your source with a page number.

> **Q:** What was Netflix's total revenue for fiscal year 2023?
>
> **A:** Netflix reported total revenue of $33.7 billion for fiscal year 2023, as stated in the consolidated statements of operations in their 2023 10-K filing.
>
> **Supporting Facts:**
> Source: Netflix 10-K FY2023, pg. 57

---

### Medium

Requires digging — a calculation, a cross-section comparison, or a figure not directly stated. Show your work in the Supporting Facts.

> **Q:** What was Netflix's operating margin for fiscal year 2023, and how did it change from the prior year?
>
> **A:** Netflix's operating margin for fiscal year 2023 was 20.6%, calculated by dividing operating income ($7.0 billion) by total revenue ($33.7 billion). This is an improvement from 17.8% in fiscal year 2022 ($5.6 billion operating income / $31.6 billion revenue), reflecting margin expansion driven by cost discipline and the paid sharing rollout. Neither figure is directly stated — both require calculation across the income statement.
>
> **Supporting Facts:**
> - Source: Netflix 10-K FY2023, pgs. 57, 59
> - Total revenue (FY2023): $33.7B (pg. 57)
> - Operating income (FY2023): $7.0B (pg. 57)
> - Total revenue (FY2022): $31.6B (pg. 59)
> - Operating income (FY2022): $5.6B (pg. 59)

---

### Hard

Draws on **at least 2 filings**. You're not limited to your 3 assigned companies — look up additional filings as needed (see `2.0_all_companies.csv` for the full list). The more filings you use, the stronger the claim you can make.

Good Hard questions reveal patterns, comparisons, or trends that only become visible across multiple documents. They should not be narrowly about just your 3 assigned companies.

**Filing date requirement:** every filing cited must be dated October 2023 or newer. Reviewers check this.

Hard questions can be:
- **Descriptive:** What consistently appears across filings of this type?
- **Comparative:** What varies meaningfully across companies?
- **Temporal:** How do macroeconomic events show up across filings over time?

> **Example (comparative):**
>
> **Q:** Among major energy companies, which firms use financial derivatives to hedge commodity price exposure, and what does this reveal about their risk management approach?
>
> **A:** ExxonMobil (FY2023 10-K) explicitly states it does not use financial derivatives to hedge commodity price risk, relying on its integrated business model as a natural hedge. Sunrun (FY2023) discloses interest rate swaps to manage financing risk on solar asset portfolios. Plug Power (FY2023) carries significant commodity exposure with limited hedging disclosure. Larger integrated companies rely on operational diversification; smaller capital-intensive firms are more exposed and less systematically hedged.
>
> **Supporting Facts:**
> - ExxonMobil 10-K FY2023, pg. 45: "The Company does not use financial derivative instruments to hedge commodity price risk, relying instead on its integrated business model as a natural hedge."
> - Sunrun 10-K FY2023, pg. 67: Discloses use of interest rate swaps to manage financing risk on solar asset portfolios.
> - Plug Power 10-K FY2023: No hedging policy found in risk factors or notes to financial statements. [Reviewed pgs. 38–52.]

> **Example (temporal):**
>
> **Q:** How did the rising interest rate environment of 2022–2023 manifest across 10-K risk factor disclosures for U.S. financial services companies?
>
> **A:** JPMorgan Chase (FY2023), Bank of America (FY2023), and Ally Financial (FY2023) all prominently feature rising interest rates in their risk factor and performance sections. JPMorgan disclosed NII of $89.3B (up from $66.3B in FY2022) while flagging duration risk. Bank of America reported ~$100B in unrealized losses on its held-to-maturity portfolio. Ally flagged margin compression as deposit costs rose faster than loan yields. All three include +100bps NII sensitivity analyses, reflecting a consistent industry-wide adjustment to a higher-rate regime.
>
> **Supporting Facts:**
> - JPMorgan Chase 10-K FY2023, pg. 163: NII $89.3B (FY2023) vs. $66.3B (FY2022); duration risk in securities portfolio disclosed.
> - Bank of America 10-K FY2023, pg. 54: ~$100B in unrealized losses on held-to-maturity portfolio.
> - Ally Financial 10-K FY2023, pg. 47: Margin compression risk flagged as deposit costs rose faster than loan yields.
> - Interest rate sensitivity (+100bps NII): JPMorgan (pg. 171), Bank of America (pg. 61), Ally (pg. 49).

## Supporting Facts

Every Q&A needs a Supporting Facts section. This lets reviewers verify your work without opening the filings themselves.

- **Easy** — page citation only.
- **Medium** — page citation plus every input value used in any calculation.
- **Hard** — for each company, quote or paraphrase the exact evidence you're relying on, with filing and page. If something is *absent* from a filing and that matters to your answer, note where you looked:
  ```
  [No going concern qualification found in auditor's report or risk factors; reviewed pgs. 38–52.]
  ```

---

## Quality Standards

Before submitting, check each Q&A against this list:

- **Factual only** — questions must be answerable from the documents. No opinion questions unless the opinion is explicitly stated in the filing.
- **Supporting Facts complete** — see above for what's required per category.
- **No PII** — no personal names, contact info, or identifying details. Refer to individuals by role ("the CFO") rather than name.
- **Additional Info** — if your answer uses a non-standard definition or industry convention not obvious from the Q&A itself, add a brief note:
  > Additional Info: "Free cash flow" is calculated here as operating cash flow minus capex, consistent with Boeing's IR disclosures. The 10-K does not use this term directly.

## After You Submit

Once you return your CSV and PDFs, a reviewer will check each Q&A and assign a rating:
- **2 (Accept):** you're paid and done.
- **1 (Revise):** minor issue — the reviewer will propose a correction. We may reach out to ask if you accept it or want to push back. You'll be paid for any rebuttal time.
- **0 (Reject):** factual error or missing source — you'll need to revise and resubmit at no additional pay.
