## Question Types

We need three kinds of Q&A pairs. Your CSV rows are pre-labeled Easy, Medium, or Hard in the `question_category` column — just write the Q&A that fits the label on each row.

### Easy

Easy questions ask about a single, clearly stated fact. A reader should be able to find the answer by skimming the filing. All you need in your Supporting Facts is the page number where the fact appears.

**Example:**

> **Q:** What was Netflix's total revenue for fiscal year 2023?
>
> **A:** Netflix reported total revenue of $33.7 billion for fiscal year 2023, as stated in the consolidated statements of operations in their 2023 10-K filing.
>
> **Supporting Facts:**
> Source: Netflix 10-K FY2023, pg. 57

---

### Medium

Medium questions require a bit more digging — a calculation, a comparison across sections, or a figure that isn't directly stated anywhere. Your Supporting Facts need to include every number you used, so a reviewer can verify your math without opening the filing.

**Example:**

> **Q:** What was Netflix's operating margin for fiscal year 2023, and how did it change from the prior year?
>
> **A:** Netflix's operating margin for fiscal year 2023 was 20.6%, calculated by dividing operating income ($7.0 billion) by total revenue ($33.7 billion). This is an improvement from 17.8% in fiscal year 2022 ($5.6 billion operating income / $31.6 billion revenue), reflecting margin expansion driven by cost discipline and the paid sharing rollout. Neither figure is directly stated — both require calculation across the income statement.
>
> **Supporting Facts:**
> - Source: Netflix 10-K FY2023, pgs. 57, 59
> - Total revenue (FY2023): $33.7B (pg. 57)
> - Operating income (FY2023): $7.0B (pg. 57)
> - Total revenue (FY2022): $31.6B (pg. 59)
> - Operating income (FY2022): $5.6B (pg. 59)

---

### Hard

Hard questions draw on information from **at least 3 filings**. The goal is to surface patterns, comparisons, or trends that only become visible when you look across multiple documents — things you couldn't answer from just one company's report.

You're not limited to the companies in your task set. You can (and should!) pull filings from other companies in `2.0_all_companies.csv` if it makes for a stronger question. The more filings you draw from, the stronger the claim you can make — a question backed by 5+ filings can make a sector-level argument that a 3-filing question can't.

Hard questions can take a few different forms:
- **Comparative:** How do these companies differ on X?
- **Descriptive:** What shows up consistently across filings of this type?
- **Temporal:** How did some macroeconomic event (rising interest rates, a recession) show up across filings over time?

> **Note:** page numbers in the examples below are illustrative — your submissions must include accurate page numbers from the actual filings you use.

**Example 1 (comparative):**

> **Q:** Among major energy companies, which firms use financial derivatives to hedge commodity price exposure, and what does this reveal about their risk management approach?
>
> **A:** ExxonMobil (FY2023 10-K) explicitly states it does not use financial derivatives to hedge commodity price risk, relying on its integrated business model as a natural hedge. Sunrun (FY2023) discloses interest rate swaps to manage financing risk on solar asset portfolios. Plug Power (FY2023) carries significant commodity exposure with limited hedging disclosure. Larger integrated companies rely on operational diversification; smaller capital-intensive firms tend to be more exposed and less systematically hedged.
>
> **Supporting Facts:**
> - ExxonMobil 10-K FY2023, pg. 45: "The Company does not use financial derivative instruments to hedge commodity price risk, relying instead on its integrated business model as a natural hedge."
> - Sunrun 10-K FY2023, pg. 67: Discloses use of interest rate swaps to manage financing risk on solar asset portfolios.
> - Plug Power 10-K FY2023: No hedging policy found in risk factors or notes to financial statements. [Reviewed pgs. 38–52.]

**Example 2 (temporal):**

> **Q:** How did the rising interest rate environment of 2022–2023 manifest across 10-K risk factor disclosures for U.S. financial services companies?
>
> **A:** JPMorgan Chase (FY2023), Bank of America (FY2023), and Ally Financial (FY2023) all prominently feature rising interest rates in their risk factor and performance sections. JPMorgan disclosed NII of $89.3B (up from $66.3B in FY2022) while flagging duration risk. Bank of America reported ~$100B in unrealized losses on its held-to-maturity portfolio. Ally flagged margin compression as deposit costs rose faster than loan yields. All three include +100bps NII sensitivity analyses, reflecting a consistent industry-wide adjustment to a higher-rate regime.
>
> **Supporting Facts:**
> - JPMorgan Chase 10-K FY2023, pg. 163: NII $89.3B (FY2023) vs. $66.3B (FY2022); duration risk in securities portfolio disclosed.
> - Bank of America 10-K FY2023, pg. 54: ~$100B in unrealized losses on held-to-maturity portfolio.
> - Ally Financial 10-K FY2023, pg. 47: Margin compression risk flagged as deposit costs rose faster than loan yields.
> - Interest rate sensitivity (+100bps NII): JPMorgan (pg. 171), Bank of America (pg. 61), Ally (pg. 49).

## Supporting Facts

Every Q&A needs a `supporting_facts` entry. This is what lets a reviewer check your work without having to open the filings themselves. Think of it as showing your work — the more clearly you lay out your evidence, the easier it is for the reviewer to give you a 2 (Accept) without any back-and-forth.

What you need to include depends on the question type:

- **Easy** — just the source citation with the page number. That's it! The reviewer will open that page to confirm the fact is there.

  ```
  Supporting Facts:
    Source: Netflix 10-K FY2023, pg. 57
  ```

- **Medium** — source with page numbers, plus every number you used in any calculation. The reviewer should be able to reproduce your math using only what you provide here, without opening anything.

  ```
  Supporting Facts:
    Source: Netflix 10-K FY2023, pgs. 57, 59
    Total revenue (FY2023): $33.7B (pg. 57)
    Operating income (FY2023): $7.0B (pg. 57)
    Total revenue (FY2022): $31.6B (pg. 59)
    Operating income (FY2022): $5.6B (pg. 59)
  ```

- **Hard** — for each company you cite, reproduce the specific data point or language your answer relies on, along with the filing name and page number. If something is *absent* from a filing and that matters to your answer (e.g., "no going concern qualification found"), document where you looked and confirm nothing was there.

  ```
  Supporting Facts:
    ExxonMobil 10-K FY2023, pg. 45: "The Company does not use financial derivative
      instruments to hedge commodity price risk..."
    Sunrun 10-K FY2023, pg. 67: Discloses use of interest rate swaps to manage
      financing risk on solar asset portfolios.
    Plug Power 10-K FY2023: No hedging policy found in risk factors or notes to
      financial statements. [Reviewed pgs. 38–52.]
  ```

See the examples in [Question Types](#question-types) above for how this looks in practice.

## Additional Info

The `additional_info` column is for anything a reviewer would need to understand your answer that isn't covered by the Supporting Facts. This is different from Supporting Facts — Supporting Facts is about *evidence* (where the numbers came from), while Additional Info is about *context* (how to interpret them).

Use it when your answer relies on an industry convention, a non-standard definition, or a methodological assumption that isn't obvious from the Q&A itself.

**Example:**

> Additional Info: "Free cash flow" is calculated here as operating cash flow minus capital expenditures, consistent with Boeing's investor relations disclosures. The 10-K does not use this term directly.

If none of this applies to your Q&A, you can leave `additional_info` blank.

## Before You Submit

Quick checklist before you submitting your Q/A:

- **Factual only** — every question should be answerable from the documents. We don't want opinion questions unless an opinion is explicitly stated in the filing.
- **Supporting Facts complete** — check that you've included everything required for your question's category (see [Supporting Facts](#supporting-facts) above).
- **No PII** — please don't include personal names, contact info, or identifying details about individuals. If a filing mentions an executive by name in passing, just refer to their role (e.g., "the CFO") instead. Reviewers will flag PII, so catching it yourself saves you a revision round.
- **Time logged** — make sure the `time_spent` column is filled in for every row you completed.
